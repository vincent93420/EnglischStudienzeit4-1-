About the Author

Nic Stone is an American author. She writes books for young adults (YA) and teenagers.

She wants to bring different cultures and diverse voices to literature. Dear Martin was her first novel, published in 2017. It became a bestseller and started many important conversations about society.

Summary

The story follows Justyce McAllister, a 17-year-old Black student. He is very smart, attends a private school, and wants to go to Yale University.

One night, Justyce tries to help his ex-girlfriend, but a police officer arrests him violently because of his skin color. This unfair event changes Justyce. To cope with his anger and confusion, he starts writing a diary in the form of letters to Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

Justyce tries to see if Dr. King's peaceful teachings still work in the modern world.

Main Themes

The book explores difficult but real topics:

  • Racism & Racial Profiling
  • Police Brutality
  • Identity & Belonging
  • Privilege
  • Social Justice

Main Characters

Justyce McAllister

The protagonist. He is observant, intelligent, and struggles to find his place in society.

Manny Rivers

Justyce’s best friend. He is wealthy and Black, but he often ignores racism to fit in.

SJ (Sarah-Jane)

Justyce's debate partner and friend. She is Jewish and speaks out against racism.

Jared Christensen

A white classmate. At the start, he makes racist jokes and does not understand Justyce's struggle.

Key Quote

“You can't change how people look at you, but you can change how you look at yourself.”
— Meaning: Self-respect is more important than the opinions of others.
